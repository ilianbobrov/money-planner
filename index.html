Wealthsimple account guide
TFSA$7,000/yr · use first
All growth and withdrawals are completely tax-free. On Wealthsimple this is your default account. Max this out before anything else — even if you only invest $583/month you're maxing the annual limit. Best for: ETFs, stocks you plan to hold long-term.
RRSP18% of income · cuts your tax bill
Contributions are deducted from your taxable income — meaning if you earn $71,500 and put $5,000 in RRSP, you're taxed as if you earned $66,500. Especially powerful for you since you're in a higher bracket from two jobs. Withdrawals are taxed (ideally in retirement when your income is lower). Best for: long-term retirement savings.
Crypto accountSpeculative · keep under 5–10%
Wealthsimple has a separate crypto account for BTC, ETH, SOL, etc. Gains are taxable (50% inclusion rate in Canada). Keep this a small slice of your overall portfolio — it's high risk, high reward. Never put money here you can't afford to lose.
